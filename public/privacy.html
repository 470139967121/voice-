Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

1. Information We Collect

Account information — email address, display name, and profile photos you provide when creating or updating your account.

Device information — device manufacturer, model, operating system version, screen resolution, RAM, and app version. This helps us diagnose issues and enforce our policies.

Network information — IP address, ISP, ASN, and approximate geolocation derived from your IP address. This is used for safety enforcement and abuse prevention.

Usage logs — app actions, page views, errors, and crash reports. These help us monitor performance and fix problems.

Sign-in identities — if you link multiple sign-in methods (Google, Apple, Email) to your account, we store the associated provider identifiers (such as email addresses and Apple identifiers) in an identity map alongside your account to enable multi-provider sign-in.

Voice chat data — connection metadata only (e.g., join/leave times, connection quality). We do not record audio.

Age verification data — your date of birth (entered at sign-up) and, if you choose to access 18+ features (private messages, gacha), a temporary copy of a government-issued ID (passport, driver's licence, or national ID card). The ID image is reviewed by a human admin and is permanently deleted as soon as a decision is recorded — we do not retain the image after review. Only the verification status, decision timestamp, and ID type (e.g. "passport") are kept on your account afterwards.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Provide, maintain, and improve our services
  • Enforce community standards and our policies
  • Device and network banning for user safety
  • Error monitoring and debugging
  • Resolve your identity across multiple sign-in providers
  • Prevent multi-account abuse
  • Verify that users of 18+ gated features (private messages, gacha) are 18 or older, in line with Apple App Store content guidelines and UK Online Safety Act requirements

3. Data Storage & Retention

  • Live logs: stored in Firestore with configurable retention (default 48 hours)
  • Archived logs: stored in R2 with 90-day retention
  • User data: stored in Firestore, backed up to R2 with 7-day backup retention
  • Images and media: stored on Cloudflare R2 CDN
  • Age verification ID images: stored on Cloudflare R2 CDN ONLY until an admin records a decision (approve, reject, or DOB modify). The image is deleted as part of the same admin action. Worst-case retention is bounded by the admin queue review time, typically minutes to a few hours. Only the verification status, ID type, and decision timestamp persist after deletion.

4. Device & Network Banning

We may ban specific devices or network addresses to enforce our policies and protect our community. Bans may be temporary or permanent, and may be applied automatically when an account is suspended.

10. Age Verification

Why we collect this. ShyTalk's private messages and gacha features are 18+. Apple App Store guideline 1.1.4 and the UK Online Safety Act both require us to make a reasonable effort to confirm a user is 18 or older before granting access to those features.

How verification works. The 18+ features are gated behind a server-side flag on your account. To unlock them, you upload one government-issued ID image (passport, driver's licence, or national ID card) to a private review queue. An admin compares the date of birth on the ID against the date of birth on your account, then records a decision: approve, reject, or modify the date of birth on file.

Image lifetime. Your ID image is uploaded directly to encrypted storage (Cloudflare R2) using a short-lived signed URL — the upload bypasses our backend so the image never touches our application servers. The image is permanently deleted as part of the same admin action that records the decision; we do not retain copies, backups, or thumbnails. The only persistent record afterwards is the verification status, ID type (e.g. "passport"), decision timestamp, and an audit-log entry naming the admin who decided.

Sub-18 users. If your account's date of birth indicates you are under 18, you cannot enter the verification flow. The 18+ features remain disabled and you cannot override this by submitting an ID. If you believe the date of birth on your account is wrong, please contact support — we will not accept ID submissions to override the date of birth on file. Once you turn 18, the verification flow becomes available automatically.

Reverting verification. If an admin reviewing your ID determines that the date of birth on file is wrong AND that you are under 18, your verification status is reverted, the 18+ features are disabled, and any existing private-message threads on your account are locked until you reach 18. The admin records a written reason for this decision in the audit log, and you receive a system message explaining the change.

Legal basis (UK GDPR / EU GDPR). We process this data on the basis of legal obligation (Apple guideline 1.1.4, UK Online Safety Act) and legitimate interest (preventing minors from accessing 18+ features). You have all the rights described in section 6 above, including the right to request deletion of your verification records.

Non-prod environments. ShyTalk operates a development environment for internal testing. In that environment the verification flow runs end-to-end but the ID image is treated as test data — please do NOT submit a real government-issued ID in the dev environment. Upload any non-sensitive image (a screenshot, a photo of a book, etc.) to exercise the flow.
